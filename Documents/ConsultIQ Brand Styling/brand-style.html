01 — Design Principles

What guides every decision

Six principles govern all design choices within the ConsultIQ system. They are not aspirational — they are mandatory.

Clarity

Every interface element must communicate its purpose instantly. Labels, data displays, and interactions require no guesswork.

Consistency

Visual patterns, component behaviours, and terminology must remain uniform across all views and user roles.

Authority

The interface projects confidence and competence. Design reinforces trust in recommendations and scoring outputs.

Simplicity

Complexity is hidden behind clean interfaces. The engine's sophistication is felt in results, not imposed through clutter.

Responsiveness

Layouts adapt gracefully to desktop, tablet, and mobile viewports without loss of functionality or legibility.

Accessibility

The system is usable by individuals of diverse abilities. No feature is inaccessible due to a design choice.

14 — Logo & Brand Mark

Logo usage

The ConsultIQ logo consists of a wordmark (“consultIQ”) combined with a magnifying glass and brain icon, representing intelligent search and analysis. The logo should be used consistently to maintain brand recognition.

Full-colour on light backgrounds
“consult” in neutral grey (#4A5568), “IQ” and icon in blue (#3A6EA5).
Monochrome on dark backgrounds
White or light monochrome version to ensure contrast and visibility.

Restricted Usage

The logo colours must not be replaced with the primary palette (e.g., deep navy or gold), except for approved monochrome variations. Do not alter the proportions between the icon and the wordmark.
